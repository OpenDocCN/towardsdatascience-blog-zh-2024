I’ve always been more into video games than sports, and back in the early 2000s, being into video games didn’t make you a cool streamer or e-sport player; it made you a nerd.

At some point in my life, by chance, I fell in love with weightlifting. I like to define weightlifting as a sport for nerds: it is an optimization problem where you have to balance training and diet, and you can see your progress in numbers; if you lift more this week than last week, you are getting better.

My journey had its fair share of stops and starts. Injuries, moving, a pandemic, and losing interest got in the way. But a month ago, I got inspired to set up a gym at home: a rack, dumbbells, a barbell, weights, and a bench. And just like that, I was back in action.

The Challenge

In the past, I trained in regular gyms but never with a personal trainer. I thought they were too expensive and didn’t see how they could help more than I could help myself. That was a mistake. Training alone led to injuries and I stopped getting better, staying just average. Over time, I learned that having a good coach is really important (in life, in general, in any field).
